Abstract

Dynamic scene reconstruction and real-time novel view synthesis have always been challenging tasks. The explicit representation based on Gaussian Splatting has demonstrated impressive performance on reconstruction quality and rendering efficiency. However, explicit point-based rendering suffers from geometric degradation when the input views is decreased. The motion of Gaussian primitives exacerbates the noise, further affecting the rendering quality. We propose DRGaussian, a deformable Gaussian splatting framework for dynamic scene reconstruction. We design effective deformation constraints to smooth the motion field of Gaussian splats. To mitigate degradation in scene geometry, we propose a depth regularization loss to help Gaussians learn structure details of objects based on patch-level and image-level depth supervision. We conducted experiments on multiple public datasets containing both synthetic and real scenes. Our model outperforms state-of-the-art methods, achieving better results on color and depth reconstruction for dynamic scenes.
